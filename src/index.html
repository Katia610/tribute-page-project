Father Pedro Opeka

A lifelong fight against extreme poverty in Madagascar

Father Pedro Opeka, surrounded by children in one of his Akamasoa villages

Biography and Mission in Madagascar

  • 1948: Born in Buenos Aires, Argentina, the son of Slovene refugees
  • 1966: Joined the seminary of Lazarists to become priest
  • 1968: Went to Ljubljana University in Slovenia to further his training
  • 1970: First visit to Madagascar where he worked as a bricklayer in a local parish
  • 1972: Joined the Catholic Institute of Paris in France to continue his theology studies
  • 1975: Ordained priest in Buenos Aires and nominated to head a rural parish in southeast Madagascar
  • 1976-1989: Vicar of the parish of Vaingandrano in the southeast of Madagascar where he shared the life of the poor farmers
  • 1989: Invited to Anatananarivo to become Head of the Saint Vincent-de-Paul Theological college. This same year that he discovered the garbage dump of the town. There he found thousands of children and parents living on a mountain of trash who were fighting for food among dogs and pigs. At this moment, when he faced this apocalyptic scene of human degradation in extreme poverty, he thought “here we mustn’t speak, we must act”. He talked to these people and visited them every day for six months before he managed to convince 70 families to quit the garbage dump in order to create a new village on a land located 60 km from the town, given by the authorities
  • 1990: Creation of the humanitarian association Akamasoa ('good friend' in Malagasy) which aims to give back a human dignity to families that extreme poverty has forced to live in inhuman conditions. Father Pedro is convinced that this dignity could be find again if there is a structure and a human and spiritual environment which could help people to rebuild themselves. That is to say: housing, schools and employment. Families and Father Pedro built together houses and the first classrooms and set up rules of life in this newly created community
  • 2014: Akamasoa celebrated 25 years of existence. Over this time, Akamasoa has helped more than 500,000 Malagasy people
  • Today: 25,000 people are living in 18 villages built by the Association. More than 3,000 houses have been built, as well as schools, dispensaries, and sport structures in each village. 12,160 children have been enrolled in these schools

International recognition

Father Opeka has received countless honours and awards, including the following:
  • 2007: Father Opeka becomes knight of the French Legion of Honour. This award recognises the ongoing fight led by Father Opeka in Madagascar against extreme poverty
  • 2009: Father Opeka receives the Golden Order for Services, the highest national decoration of Slovenia
  • 2012 / 2013 / 2015 / 2021: Father Opeka was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize with the support of the Slovene government and also parliamentaries of France, Madagascar, the European Union and also Canadian, Argentinean, Australian, Monegasque organisations and associations.
